"If you, as a woman, a Catholic woman in Spain, marry a civil, not in a church, and your husband divorces you, can you then marry again, but then in a Catholic church?"

schedule 00:14

So the short version is: yes, it's possible, but it involves a bit of a process.

In the Catholic Church, if your first marriage wasn't a Catholic sacramental marriage—meaning it wasn't done in the church and wasn't recognized as a sacrament—then the Church generally views it as a civil contract. That means after a civil divorce, you can often marry in the Catholic Church because, in the Church's eyes, you weren't sacramentally married before.

You'll likely need to speak with a priest and go through some pastoral guidance or paperwork to confirm the situation, but generally, yes, you can marry again in the Church after a civil-only marriage and divorce.
